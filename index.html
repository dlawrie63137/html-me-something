Confessions of an Airshow Junkie:

I Love to Watch 'Em Fly!

The Beginning of an Obsession:

Ever since I can remember I've loved to watch airplanes fly. Maybe it was because I grew up in the flight path of small airport and nearly always had planes flying overhead. Or, maybe it was because my father had a job with a small charter airline, Capitol Air, when I was young. One of the perks of a career in the airline industry is the ability to travel for next to nothing. Growing up I had the opportunity to travel to many places, including England, Scotland, Germany, Italy, and Hawaii.

Lucky me, my dad also had a few friends who had earned their private licenses and I was also able to fly on smaller General Aviation aircraft, like the Cessna 150, the Cessna 172, and the Beech Bonanza, on more than one occasion. I fell in love with flying and would jump at the chance to climb into just about any aircraft for a ride. Eventually, my dad was able to obtain his pilot license and these opportunities became more frequent for several years. To this day I love to fly, be it in a major commercial jet, a regional puddle jumper, or small GA aircraft.

The Next Chapter:

This led me to follow a career in the airline industry, first as an Avionics Technician with the Navy, then in a similar capacity with TWA in St. Louis. I spent six years in the Navy, stationed at VRC-40 in Norfolk working on the Grumman C-2A(R). During this time I was able to travel to several new destinations including the Azore Islands... Rota, Spain... Sigonella, Sicily... Chania, Crete... Tel Aviv, Israel... Manama, Bahrain... Halifax, Nova Scotia... and San Juan, Puerto Rico.

I'm always amazed that the larger jets are able to get off the ground. I remember when I was in Bahrain with the Navy, our workspace was a surpringly short distance from the end of the runway. I would watch as the Gulf Air 747s would start to slowly lumber down the runway and I would think to myself, "There's no way that something that big is going to get off the ground, it can barely even get moving!" Without fail, by the opposite end it would be airborne.

And So, Today:

These days I don't get many opportunities to fly anymore. My short career in the airline industry ended with the sale of TWA to American Airlines in early 2001, followed by the 9/11 attacks later that year. My dad is in his mid-eighties and no longer flying. So to scratch my flying itch I live vicariously through the many airshow pilots that tour the country each year.

My favorite airshow performers (and maybe everybody else's also) are the Blue Angels. The Blue Angels are the United States Navy Flight Demonstration Squadron, and are arguably among the best pilots in the world. I've had the pleasure of seeing their performances several times now in shows in St. Louis, Cape Girardeau, Davenport, Iowa and Ypsilanti, Mi.

You'll find more than just military jets at your local airshow, though. Performers include everything from old Stearman bi-planes and World War I replica aircraft to the Breitling Flight Demonstration Team. And let's be honest, this looks like it would be fun to try, right?

Somebody's having fun in a Stearman Biplane.
